k-Nearest Neighbors

Posted on 2023-03-14 Edited on 2023-03-18 In Machine Learning

Introduction

kNN a is a simple and widely used machine learning algorithm for classification and regression.Building the model consists only of storing the training dataset. To make a prediction for a new data point, the algorithm finds the closest data points in the training dataset—its “nearest neighbors.”

For classification problems, the predicted label is typically the mode (i.e., most common) label among the k neighbors; for regression problems, the predicted value is typically the mean (i.e., average) value of the target variable among the k neighbors.

Distance Metric

Generally, kNN uses a distance metric, such as Euclidean or Manhattan distance, to determine the similarity between data points.

Manhattan distance, also known as taxicab distance, is a metric used to measure the distance between two points in a coordinate system. It is named after the grid-like layout of the streets in Manhattan, where the shortest distance between two points is often along the perpendicular grid lines rather than a straight line.
